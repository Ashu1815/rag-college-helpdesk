COLLEGE HELPDESK - FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Q: What is the minimum attendance requirement to sit for exams?
A: Students must maintain a minimum of 75% attendance in each subject to be eligible to sit for the semester-end examinations. Students with attendance between 65% and 75% may apply for condonation through the department office with valid supporting documents.

Q: How do I apply for a re-evaluation of my answer sheet?
A: Re-evaluation requests must be submitted online through the student portal within 7 days of result declaration. A fee of Rs. 500 per subject is charged. Results of re-evaluation are typically declared within 3 weeks.

Q: What is the process for getting a bonafide certificate?
A: Submit a request through the student portal under "Certificate Requests" or visit the academic office with your ID card. Processing time is 3-5 working days. There is no fee for the first copy in an academic year.

Q: How many backlogs are allowed to carry forward to the next year?
A: Students are allowed to carry forward a maximum of 4 backlog subjects to the next academic year. Students exceeding this limit will not be promoted and must clear backlogs before re-registering for the next year's subjects.

Q: What is the procedure for hostel room allocation?
A: Hostel allocation is done through the online hostel portal at the start of each academic year, based on merit and seniority. Existing residents get priority for room retention if fee dues are cleared before the deadline.

Q: How do I request a transcript or migration certificate?
A: Transcripts and migration certificates can be requested through the Examination Cell. Submit the online form along with the required fee (Rs. 200 for transcript, Rs. 500 for migration certificate). Processing takes 7-10 working days.

Q: What is the grievance redressal process for exam-related issues?
A: Students can file a grievance through the Student Grievance Portal or submit a written complaint to the Controller of Examinations within 15 days of the issue arising. The grievance committee reviews and responds within 2 weeks.

Q: Can I change my elective subject after the semester has started?
A: Elective changes are only permitted within the first 2 weeks of the semester, subject to seat availability and approval from the Head of Department.

Q: What happens if I miss a mid-semester exam?
A: Students who miss a mid-semester exam due to medical or other valid emergencies must submit supporting documents to the department within 3 working days to be eligible for a makeup test, subject to faculty approval.

Q: How is the final grade calculated?
A: The final grade is typically calculated as 30% internal assessment (assignments, quizzes, mid-semester exams) and 70% end-semester examination, unless otherwise specified in the subject's course structure.
