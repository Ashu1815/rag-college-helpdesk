GENERAL COLLEGE FAQ - EXTENDED

Q: What documents are required at the time of admission?
A: Students must submit original mark sheets of the qualifying examination, transfer certificate, migration certificate, category certificate (if applicable), passport-size photographs, and proof of address at the time of admission.

Q: How can I pay my semester fees?
A: Fees can be paid online through the student portal using net banking, UPI, or debit/credit card, or offline through the designated bank branch using the fee challan generated from the portal.

Q: What is the last date for fee payment each semester?
A: Fees must be paid within the first two weeks of each semester. A late fee of Rs. 50 per day is charged for payments made after the deadline, up to a maximum grace period of one month.

Q: Is there an installment option for fee payment?
A: Yes, students facing financial difficulty can apply for a fee installment plan through the Accounts Office, splitting the semester fee into two installments with prior approval.

Q: How do I get a duplicate ID card if I lose mine?
A: Report the loss to the security office and file a police complaint if required, then apply for a duplicate ID card through the student portal. A fee of Rs. 200 is charged for the duplicate card.

Q: What is the library borrowing limit for students?
A: Undergraduate students can borrow up to 4 books at a time for a period of 14 days, with one renewal allowed if no other student has requested the same book.

Q: What happens if I return a library book late?
A: A fine of Rs. 2 per day per book is charged for late returns. Access to further borrowing is suspended until outstanding fines are cleared.

Q: Can I access the library e-resources from home?
A: Yes, the library provides remote access to e-journals and e-books through the institutional login on the library portal, available to all enrolled students.

Q: How do I get access to the campus WiFi?
A: WiFi credentials are provided to students via their college email at the start of each academic year. In case of login issues, contact the IT helpdesk with your student ID.

Q: What is the process to request a leave of absence for a semester?
A: Submit a Leave of Absence application through the Dean of Academics' office, along with supporting documents. Approval is subject to maintaining a minimum academic standing and the leave cannot exceed two consecutive semesters.

Q: How do I register for courses each semester?
A: Course registration is done online through the student portal during the designated registration window, typically one week before the semester begins. Late registration is allowed with department approval and a late fee.

Q: What is the maximum credit load I can register for in a semester?
A: Students can register for a maximum of 26 credits per semester under the normal load. Students with a CGPA above 8.5 may apply for an additional 2 credits with department approval.

Q: How do I drop a course after registering?
A: Courses can be dropped without penalty within the first two weeks of the semester through the student portal. Dropping after this period requires approval and may reflect as a "W" (withdrawal) on the transcript.

Q: What is the procedure for changing my branch/department?
A: Branch change requests are considered at the end of the first year based on CGPA and seat availability in the target department. Applications must be submitted to the Academic Office within the specified window.

Q: How can I access my semester grade card?
A: Grade cards are published on the student portal within 30 days of the last exam. A physical copy can be requested from the Examination Cell for an additional fee.

Q: What is the procedure to apply for anti-ragging related complaints?
A: Complaints can be filed anonymously or with identification through the Anti-Ragging Committee's online portal, or by contacting the 24x7 anti-ragging helpline number displayed on campus notice boards.

Q: What disciplinary actions can be taken for violation of the code of conduct?
A: Depending on severity, actions range from a written warning, fines, suspension from classes or hostel, to expulsion from the college, as decided by the Disciplinary Committee.

Q: How do I book a seat in the college gym or sports facilities?
A: Sports facilities can be booked through the Sports Office or the campus app, subject to availability. Priority is given to students representing the college in inter-college tournaments.

Q: Can I join more than one student club or society?
A: Yes, students can join multiple clubs, but leadership positions are generally limited to one club per student to avoid scheduling conflicts.

Q: How do I start a new student club?
A: Submit a proposal to the Student Activities Office, including a list of at least 15 interested members and a faculty advisor willing to mentor the club. Approval is granted by the Dean of Student Affairs.

Q: What is the procedure to apply for an internship through the college?
A: Internship opportunities are posted on the Placement Cell portal. Students apply directly through the portal, and the Placement Cell coordinates interview scheduling with participating companies.

Q: Is there a minimum CGPA requirement for internship eligibility?
A: Yes, a minimum CGPA of 6.0 is required for most internship opportunities listed through the Placement Cell, though some companies may set a higher threshold.

Q: How many placement drives can I sit for?
A: Under the "one offer" policy, once a student accepts a placement offer, they become ineligible for further placement drives unless the company falls into the specified "dream company" category.

Q: What is the process for applying to research assistantships under a professor?
A: Interested students should directly contact faculty members with an updated CV and area of interest. Some departments also post open assistantship positions on the department notice board each semester.

Q: How do I submit my final year major project?
A: Final year projects are submitted through the department portal along with a printed report, following the format guidelines issued by the department. A viva-voce is scheduled after submission.

Q: What is the penalty for late submission of assignments?
A: Late assignment submissions are penalized at 10% of the awarded marks per day late, up to a maximum of 3 days, after which the assignment is not accepted for grading.

Q: Can I resubmit an assignment for improved grading?
A: Resubmission policy varies by faculty; some allow one resubmission within a week of receiving feedback, at the discretion of the course instructor.

Q: What is the process for applying for a transfer to another affiliated college?
A: Transfer requests must be submitted to the Registrar's Office with justification, along with a No Objection Certificate (NOC) from the current college and confirmation of seat availability at the receiving college.

Q: How is class attendance recorded?
A: Attendance is recorded electronically through biometric scanners or QR code check-ins at the start of each lecture, visible to students in real time on the student portal.

Q: Can I contest an incorrect attendance record?
A: Yes, discrepancies must be reported to the concerned faculty member within 3 working days along with any supporting evidence, such as a medical certificate.

Q: What is the dress code policy for classes and exams?
A: The college requires modest, presentable attire during classes and examinations. Formal wear is mandatory for placement-related events and viva examinations.

Q: How do I apply for an on-campus part-time job?
A: On-campus job openings (library assistant, lab assistant, teaching assistant) are posted on the Student Affairs portal. Applications are reviewed by the respective department.

Q: What mental health or counseling support is available on campus?
A: The college has a Student Counseling Center offering free, confidential sessions with licensed counselors. Appointments can be booked through the student portal or by walking in during office hours.

Q: How do I report an incident of harassment on campus?
A: Complaints can be submitted to the Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) through a confidential form on the student portal, or in person to any ICC member listed on the college website.

Q: What is the procedure for requesting an extension on a project deadline?
A: Extensions must be requested in writing to the project guide at least 3 days before the deadline, citing valid reasons. Approval is at the guide's discretion.

Q: How do I access previous years' question papers?
A: Previous years' question papers are available in the library's digital repository and can also be requested from the department office for reference.

Q: What are the eligibility criteria for the Dean's List?
A: Students with a semester GPA of 9.0 or above, with no active backlogs and no disciplinary record, are eligible for the Dean's List recognition each semester.

Q: How do I apply for hostel leave during weekdays?
A: Students must fill out a leave form in the hostel warden's register or through the hostel management app, specifying dates and destination, before leaving campus overnight.

Q: What is the curfew time for hostel students?
A: The hostel gate closes at 10:00 PM on weekdays and 11:00 PM on weekends. Late entries require prior written permission from the warden.

Q: Are guests allowed to stay overnight in the hostel?
A: Overnight guests are generally not permitted. Day visitors are allowed in common areas until 8:00 PM after registering with the hostel security desk.

Q: How do I report a maintenance issue in my hostel room?
A: Maintenance issues can be reported through the hostel management app or by submitting a written complaint at the warden's office, which is typically resolved within 48 hours.

Q: What is the mess fee refund policy for hostel students on leave?
A: Mess fee rebate is provided for continuous absences of 5 days or more, provided the leave was recorded in advance with the hostel office.

Q: Can I change my hostel room during the semester?
A: Room change requests are considered only in exceptional circumstances (e.g., health issues, conflicts) and must be approved by the Chief Warden.

Q: How do I access the campus health center?
A: The campus health center is open 24x7 for emergencies and 9 AM-6 PM for general consultations. Students can walk in with their ID card; no prior appointment is needed for urgent cases.

Q: Is health insurance provided to students?
A: Yes, all enrolled students are covered under a group health insurance policy for the academic year, details of which are shared during orientation.

Q: How do I apply for a bus pass for the college shuttle service?
A: Bus passes can be applied for through the Transport Office at the start of the semester, with routes and fees listed on the college website.

Q: What is the parking policy for students who bring their own vehicles?
A: Students must register their vehicle with the Security Office to obtain a parking sticker. Unregistered vehicles may be denied entry to campus parking areas.

Q: How do I get a No Objection Certificate (NOC) for a passport application?
A: NOC requests for passport applications can be submitted through the Academic Office, typically processed within 5-7 working days.

Q: What is the process for applying to a semester abroad or student exchange program?
A: Exchange program applications are coordinated through the International Relations Office. Eligibility typically requires a minimum CGPA of 7.5 and no active backlogs.

Q: How do I access academic transcripts for higher studies applications?
A: Official transcripts can be requested through the Examination Cell's online portal, with options for direct mailing to universities if required.

Q: What is the procedure for appealing a disciplinary decision?
A: Appeals must be submitted in writing to the Dean of Student Affairs within 7 days of the decision, and are reviewed by an appellate committee separate from the original disciplinary panel.

Q: How do I get involved in student government or the student council?
A: Student council elections are held at the start of each academic year. Interested students can file nominations through the Student Affairs Office during the announced nomination period.

Q: What is the procedure for requesting recommendation letters from faculty?
A: Students should approach faculty members directly, providing sufficient notice (at least 2 weeks) along with their resume and details of the program they are applying to.

Q: How are seminar and workshop attendance credits counted?
A: Many departments require students to attend a minimum number of seminars/workshops per semester as part of non-credit mandatory activities, tracked through the student portal.

Q: What is the policy on using AI tools for assignments?
A: Use of AI tools is permitted only where explicitly allowed by the course instructor; unauthorized use may be treated as academic dishonesty under the college's integrity policy.

Q: How do I report suspected academic dishonesty by another student?
A: Reports can be made confidentially to the Academic Integrity Committee via the student portal or directly to the concerned faculty member.

Q: What is the minimum CGPA required to avoid academic probation?
A: Students falling below a CGPA of 5.0 in any semester are placed on academic probation and must improve their performance in the following semester to avoid further action.

Q: How is academic probation different from suspension?
A: Academic probation is a warning status requiring improved performance, while suspension involves temporary removal from the college for a specified period due to continued poor performance or violations.

Q: What is the procedure to request a duplicate degree certificate?
A: Duplicate degree certificate requests are submitted to the Controller of Examinations along with an affidavit for the lost original and the prescribed fee.

Q: When is the convocation ceremony typically held?
A: Convocation is usually held once a year, around November-December, for students who have completed all degree requirements in the preceding academic year.

Q: How do I stay connected with the college as an alumnus?
A: Graduates can join the official Alumni Association portal, which provides networking opportunities, event invitations, and access to career services even after graduation.

Q: What is the refund policy for hostel deposit upon leaving the college?
A: The hostel security deposit is refunded within 30 days of vacating the room, provided there is no pending damage or dues, processed through the Accounts Office.

Q: Can I use lab equipment outside of scheduled class hours?
A: Access to labs outside class hours requires prior permission from the lab in-charge and is subject to availability and supervision requirements.

Q: What is the policy on using personal laptops during exams for lab-based subjects?
A: Personal laptops may be permitted for lab-based practical exams only with prior authorization and after being checked by the invigilator for unauthorized content.

Q: How do I request access to specialized software licenses for coursework?
A: Software license requests are made through the IT department, typically requiring approval from the concerned faculty member for the specific course.

Q: What is the process for filing an RTI (Right to Information) request as a student?
A: RTI requests, applicable to government-affiliated institutions, can be filed through the designated Public Information Officer listed on the college's official website.

Q: How can I track the status of my scholarship application?
A: Scholarship application status can be tracked through the "Financial Aid" section of the student portal, which is updated at each stage of processing.

Q: What is the eligibility for a merit scholarship?
A: Merit scholarships typically require a CGPA above 8.5 in the previous academic year with no backlogs, subject to available funding and department quotas.

Q: How do I apply for an education loan through the college's partner banks?
A: The Accounts Office maintains tie-ups with select banks for education loans; students can apply directly at the bank or through a dedicated counter set up during the fee payment period.

Q: What is the process for requesting course material accommodations for students with disabilities?
A: Students can register with the Office of Disability Services to request accommodations such as extended exam time, accessible material formats, or note-taking assistance.

Q: How do I get feedback on my mid-semester exam performance?
A: Mid-semester answer scripts are typically shown to students within a week of evaluation, during a scheduled "answer script review" session with the course instructor.

Q: What is the procedure to request a transcript evaluation for study abroad credit transfer?
A: Requests are processed through the Academic Office, which prepares a detailed course-equivalency document upon request, usually within 2 weeks.

Q: Can I take a course as an audit (non-credit) student?
A: Yes, subject to instructor approval and seat availability, students can audit a course without it counting toward their credit requirements or GPA.

Q: What is the policy for using recording devices in lectures?
A: Recording lectures requires prior permission from the instructor. Unauthorized recording or distribution of lecture content is a violation of the academic conduct policy.

Q: How do I apply to become a teaching assistant (TA)?
A: TA positions are advertised by departments each semester; interested students with a strong grade in the relevant course can apply through the department office.

Q: What is the process for requesting a break in registration due to medical reasons?
A: Medical leave of absence requires a certificate from a registered medical practitioner, submitted along with the leave application to the Dean of Academics.

Q: How do I get a certificate of participation for events I've organized or attended?
A: Participation certificates are issued by the Student Activities Office upon submission of event details and approval from the faculty coordinator.

Q: What is the procedure for lodging a complaint about food quality in the mess/canteen?
A: Complaints can be raised with the Mess Committee, which includes student representatives, or submitted through the feedback form available at the mess counter.

Q: How is the mess committee formed and how can I join it?
A: The mess committee comprises elected student representatives from each hostel block, chosen at the start of the academic year through a simple voting process among residents.

Q: What is the process to apply for a name correction on my degree certificate?
A: Name correction requests require submission of a gazette notification or valid government ID proof to the Examination Cell, along with the prescribed correction fee.

Q: How do I get access to career counseling services?
A: Career counseling is offered by the Placement Cell and can be booked through the student portal, covering resume building, mock interviews, and career path guidance.

Q: What is the eligibility criteria to represent the college in inter-college sports events?
A: Students must maintain a minimum attendance of 65% and have no active disciplinary cases to be eligible for selection in inter-college sports teams.

Q: How do I request sports quota consideration for admission?
A: Sports quota admissions require submission of state or national-level participation certificates to the Sports Office during the admission process for verification.

Q: What is the procedure for requesting additional hostel accommodation during exam periods for day scholars?
A: Day scholar students can request temporary hostel accommodation during exam weeks through the Hostel Office, subject to room availability and an additional short-term fee.

Q: How can I access old semester timetables for reference?
A: Archived timetables are available in the "Academic Records" section of the student portal or can be requested from the department office.

Q: What is the process for requesting an official college letterhead document for visa applications?
A: Visa-related documents such as bonafide or NOC letters on official letterhead are processed through the Academic Office, typically within 3-5 working days.

Q: How do I opt out of a mandatory extracurricular requirement due to a valid reason?
A: Exemption requests must be submitted in writing to the Student Activities Office with supporting documentation (e.g., medical condition), subject to committee approval.

Q: What is the process for requesting a change in examination center for medical reasons?
A: Requests must be submitted to the Controller of Examinations at least one week before the exam, along with a medical certificate supporting the need for a center change.

Q: How is the semester academic calendar published and can it change?
A: The academic calendar is published on the college website before the start of each semester. Minor changes (e.g., holiday adjustments) may occur with prior notice through official circulars.

Q: What is the process to request official verification of my degree for an employer?
A: Employers or third-party verification agencies can request degree verification through the Controller of Examinations office using the student's roll number and consent form.

Q: How do I apply for readmission after being expelled or having dropped out?
A: Readmission applications are reviewed by the Academic Council on a case-by-case basis, requiring a formal application and, in some cases, an interview with the department.

Q: What support is available for first-generation college students?
A: The Student Affairs Office runs a peer-mentorship program specifically for first-generation students, offering academic guidance and access to additional counseling resources.

Q: How do I request a change of guide/supervisor for my final year project?
A: Guide change requests must be submitted to the Head of Department with valid reasoning, subject to the new guide's availability and mutual agreement.

Q: What is the process for filing a complaint against unfair grading?
A: Students can first approach the concerned faculty for clarification; if unresolved, a formal grievance can be filed with the Academic Grievance Committee within 15 days of grade publication.

Q: How can I check if my elective seat allocation was successful?
A: Elective allocation results are published on the student portal under "Course Registration" within 3 days of the allocation deadline, based on the seat lottery/preference system.

Q: What is the process for requesting an official bonafide letter for a bank loan?
A: Bonafide certificates for loan purposes can be requested through the student portal, specifying the purpose, and are typically issued within 3-5 working days.

Q: How do I apply for the Student Innovation and Research Grant?
A: Applications for the innovation grant are submitted through the Research and Development Cell, requiring a project proposal and faculty mentor endorsement.

Q: What is the procedure for lost-and-found items on campus?
A: Lost items can be reported to and recovered from the campus Security Office, which maintains a lost-and-found log updated daily.

Q: How do I request access to the placement cell's alumni mentorship network?
A: Students can register interest through the Placement Cell portal, which matches them with alumni mentors based on career interest and industry domain.

Q: What is the process for organizing a college fest event or booking a venue?
A: Venue bookings and event approvals for college fests are coordinated through the Student Activities Office, requiring submission of an event proposal at least 3 weeks in advance.
